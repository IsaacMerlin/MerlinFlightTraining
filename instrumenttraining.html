Instrument Rating

Take your flying to the next level. Learn about requirements, training, costs, and the benefits of earning your Instrument Rating with Merlin Flight Training.

What is an Instrument Rating?

An Instrument Rating (IR) allows you to fly in a wider range of weather conditions, including clouds and low visibility, by relying on your aircraft's instruments. It is a critical step for pilots seeking advanced skills or a professional career.

Requirements

  • Hold at least a Private Pilot Certificate
  • Be able to read, speak, write, and understand English
  • Hold at least a current third-class medical certificate
  • Pass a knowledge (written) test
  • Pass a practical test (checkride) with an FAA examiner

Training & Flight Hours

  • Minimum 50 hours of cross-country flight time as pilot in command
  • 40 hours of actual or simulated instrument time
  • 15 hours of instrument flight training with a CFII (Certified Flight Instructor - Instrument)
  • Instrument approaches, holding procedures, and navigation by reference to instruments

Cost

The average cost for an Instrument Rating at Merlin Flight Training is $18,000. This includes aircraft rental, instructor fees, and study materials.

Why Get an Instrument Rating?

  • Fly safely in clouds and low visibility
  • Increase your confidence and skill as a pilot
  • Travel more reliably and efficiently
  • Required for most professional pilot jobs
