Commercial Pilot License

Turn your passion for flying into a career. Learn about requirements, training, costs, and the opportunities available with a Commercial Pilot License from Merlin Flight Training.

What is a Commercial Pilot License?

A Commercial Pilot License (CPL) allows you to be compensated for flying. With a CPL, you can work as a professional pilot, including jobs such as flight instructor, banner towing, aerial photography, and more.

Requirements

  • Be at least 18 years old
  • Hold at least a Private Pilot Certificate
  • Hold at least a second-class medical certificate
  • Pass a knowledge (written) test
  • Pass a practical test (checkride) with an FAA examiner

Training & Flight Hours

  • Minimum 250 hours total flight time
  • 100 hours as pilot in command
  • 50 hours of cross-country flight time
  • 20 hours of training with an instructor
  • 10 hours of solo flight time

Cost

The typical cost for Commercial Pilot training at Merlin Flight Training is $4,000 for 20 hours of training (assuming you already have the required total time). Costs may vary depending on your experience and flight time needed.

What You Can Do With a CPL

  • Get paid to fly
  • Become a flight instructor
  • Fly for charter or corporate operations
  • Tow banners or gliders
  • Conduct aerial photography or survey flights
  • Build hours toward an airline career
