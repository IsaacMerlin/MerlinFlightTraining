Private Pilot License

Your journey to becoming a pilot starts here. Learn about requirements, training, costs, and what you can do with your Private Pilot Certificate.

What is a Private Pilot License?

A Private Pilot License (PPL) allows you to fly most single-engine airplanes for personal or business travel (not for hire). It is the most common first step for aspiring pilots.

Requirements

  • Be at least 17 years old
  • Read, speak, write, and understand English
  • Hold at least a third-class medical certificate
  • Pass a knowledge (written) test
  • Pass a checkride (practical test) with an FAA examiner

Training & Flight Hours

  • Minimum 40 hours total flight time (national average is 60-70 hours)
  • 20 hours with an instructor
  • 10 hours solo flight
  • Includes cross-country, night, and instrument training

Cost

The average cost for a Private Pilot License at Merlin Flight Training is $23,000. This includes aircraft rental, instructor fees, and study materials.

What You Can Do With a PPL

  • Fly friends and family
  • Travel for business or pleasure
  • Rent or own airplanes
  • Add advanced ratings (Instrument, Commercial, etc.)
